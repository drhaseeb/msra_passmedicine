This results in axial compression of the scaphoid, with the wrist hyperextended, and radially deviated. It is important to examine for scaphoid fractures in anyone presenting with an acutely painful wrist for medico-legal reasons.

Recognising scaphoid fractures is particularly important given the unusual blood supply of the scaphoid bone. Around 80% of the blood supply is derived from the dorsal carpal branch (branch of the radial artery)<\/span>, in a retrograde manner.
