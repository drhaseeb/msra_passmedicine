[{"notes_id":"1_328","category":"2","subcategory":"3","title":"Primary open-angle glaucoma: management","body":"Glaucomas are optic neuropathies associated with raised intraocular pressure (IOP). They can be classified based on whether the peripheral iris is covering the trabecular meshwork, which is important in the drainage of aqueous humour from the anterior chamber of the eye. In open-angle glaucoma, the iris is clear of the meshwork. The trabecular network functionally offers an increased resistance to aqueous outflow, causing increased IOP.

The aim of treatment is to lower intra-ocular pressure which in turn has been shown to prevent progressive loss of visual field.
