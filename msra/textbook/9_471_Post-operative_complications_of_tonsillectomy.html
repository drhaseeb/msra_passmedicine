Haemorrhage<\/h5>
Haemorrhage is a feared complication following tonsillectomy. All post-tonsillectomy haemorrhages should be assessed by ENT<\/span>.

Primary, or reactionary haemorrhage most commonly occurs in the first 6-8 hours following surgery. It is managed by immediate return to theatre<\/span>.

Secondary haemorrhage occurs between 5 and 10 days after surgery and is often associated with a wound infection<\/span>. Treatment is usually with admission and antibiotics. Severe bleeding may require surgery.
