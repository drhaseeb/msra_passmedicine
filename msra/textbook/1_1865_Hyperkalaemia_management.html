Staging<\/h5>
The European Resuscitation Council classifies hyperkalaemia as:
  • mild: 5.5 - 5.9 mmol\/L<\/li>
  • moderate: 6.0 - 6.4 mmol\/L <\/li>
  • severe: \u2265 6.5 mmol\/L<\/span><\/li><\/ul>
    Remember that the presence of ECG<\/span> changes is important in determining the management - it should be done in all patients with new hyperkalaemia. The following changes are associated with hyperkalaemia:
    • peaked or 'tall-tented' T waves (occurs first)<\/li>
    • loss of P waves<\/li>
    • broad QRS complexes<\/li>
    • sinusoidal wave pattern<\/li><\/ul>

      Management<\/h5>
      Principles of treatment modalities<\/h6>
      Stabilisation of the cardiac membrane<\/span>
      • IV calcium gluconate
        • does NOT lower serum potassium levels<\/span><\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>
          Short-term shift in potassium from extracellular (ECF) to intracellular fluid (ICF)
          compartment<\/span>
          • combined insulin\/dextrose infusion<\/li>
          • nebulised salbutamol<\/li><\/ul>
            Removal of potassium from the body<\/span>
            • calcium resonium (orally or enema)
              • enemas are more effective than oral as potassium is secreted by the rectum<\/span><\/li><\/ul><\/li>
              • loop diuretics<\/li>
              • dialysis
                • haemofiltration\/haemodialysis should be considered for patients with AKI with persistent hyperkalaemia<\/span><\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>
                  Newer agents
                  • sodium zirconium cyclosilicate<\/span>
                    • non-absorbed inorganic cation exchanger that selectively traps potassium ions in exchange for sodium and hydrogen in the gastrointestinal tract<\/li>
                    • acts mainly in the distal small intestine and colon, where potassium concentration is highest<\/li>
                    • unlike older resins (e.g. sodium polystyrene sulfonate\/Resonium), it has better selectivity, faster onset, and improved tolerability<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>
                      Outline of practical treatment<\/h6>
                      All patients with severe hyperkalaemia (\u2265 6.5 mmol\/L) or with ECG changes should have emergency treatment<\/span>
                      • IV calcium gluconate<\/b>: to stabilise the myocardium<\/li>
                      • insulin\/dextrose infusion<\/b>: short-term shift in potassium from ECF to ICF<\/li>
                      • other treatments such as nebulised salbutamol may be given to temporarily lower the serum potassium<\/li><\/ul>
                        Further management
                        • stop exacerbating drugs e.g. ACE inhibitors<\/li>
                        • treat any underlying cause<\/li>
                        • lower total body potassium
