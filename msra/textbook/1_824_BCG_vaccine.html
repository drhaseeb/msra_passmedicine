The Greenbook currently advises that the vaccine is administered to the following groups (below is summary, please see the link for more details):
  • all infants (aged 0 to 12 months) living in areas of the UK where the annual incidence of TB is 40\/100,000 or greater<\/li>
  • all infants (aged 0 to 12 months) with a parent or grandparent who was born in a country where the annual incidence of TB is 40\/100,000 or greater. The same applies to older children but if they are 6 years old or older they require a tuberculin skin test first<\/li>
  • previously unvaccinated tuberculin-negative contacts of cases of respiratory TB <\/li>
  • previously unvaccinated, tuberculin-negative new entrants under 16 years of age who were born in or who have lived for a prolonged period (at least three months) in a country with an annual TB incidence of 40\/100,000 or greater<\/li>
  • healthcare workers<\/li>
  • prison staff<\/li>
  • staff of care home for the elderly<\/li>
  • those who work with homeless people<\/li><\/ul>
    The vaccine contains live attenuated Mycobacterium bovis. It also offers limited protection against leprosy.

    Administration
    • any person being considered for the BCG vaccine must first be given a tuberculin skin test. The only exceptions are children < 6 years old who have had no contact with tuberculosis<\/li>
    • given intradermally, normally to the lateral aspect of the left upper arm<\/li>
    • BCG can be given at the same time as other live vaccines, but if not administered simultaneously there should be a 4 week interval<\/li><\/ul>
