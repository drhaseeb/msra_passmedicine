It was previously used when aspirin was not tolerated or contraindicated but there are now a number of conditions for which clopidogrel is used in addition to aspirin, for example in patients with an acute coronary syndrome. Following the 2010 NICE technology appraisal clopidogrel is also now first-line in patients following an ischaemic stroke and in patients with peripheral arterial disease.
