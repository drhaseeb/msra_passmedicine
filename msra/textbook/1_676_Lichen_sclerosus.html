        Balanitis xerotica obliterans<\/h5>
        Balanitis xerotica obliterans (BXO) is a chronic, sclerosing inflammatory dermatosis affecting the foreskin, glans, and often the urethral meatus, considered the male equivalent of lichen sclerosus.

        It typically presents in prepubertal boys and middle-aged men with symptoms such as phimosis, white atrophic plaques, and in severe cases, urinary outflow obstruction due to meatal or urethral involvement.

        Histologically, BXO is characterised by hyperkeratosis, basal cell degeneration, and a dense lichenoid lymphocytic infiltrate with dermal fibrosis.
