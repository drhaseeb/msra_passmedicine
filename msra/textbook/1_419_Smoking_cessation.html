  • patients should be offered nicotine replacement therapy (NRT), varenicline or bupropion - NICE state that clinicians should not favour one medication over another<\/li>

    • adverse effects include nausea & vomiting, headaches and flu-like symptoms<\/li>

      • a nicotinic receptor partial agonist<\/li>

            Pregnant women<\/h5>
            NICE recommended in 2010 that all pregnant women should be tested for smoking using carbon monoxide detectors<\/span>, partly because 'some women find it difficult to say that they smoke because the pressure not to smoke during pregnancy is so intense.'<\/i>. All women who smoke, or have stopped smoking within the last 2 weeks, or those with a CO reading of 7 ppm or above should be referred to NHS Stop Smoking Services.

            Interventions
            • the first-line interventions in pregnancy should be cognitive behaviour therapy, motivational interviewing or structured self-help and support from NHS Stop Smoking Services<\/li>
