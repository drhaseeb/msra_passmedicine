It is important to recognise purpura as it can indicate the presence of serious underlying disease. Children with a new purpuric rash should be admitted immediately for investigations as it may be a sign of meningococcal septicaemia or acute lymphoblastic leukaemia<\/b>. Parenteral antibiotics should be given prior to transfer if meningococcal septicaemia is suspected.
