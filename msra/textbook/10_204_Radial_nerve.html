Path
  • In the axilla: lies posterior to the axillary artery on subscapularis, latissimus dorsi and teres major.<\/li>
  • Enters the arm between the brachial artery and the long head of triceps (medial to humerus).<\/li>
  • Spirals around the posterior surface of the humerus in the groove for the radial nerve.<\/li>
  • At the distal third of the lateral border of the humerus it then pierces the intermuscular septum and descends in front of the lateral epicondyle. <\/li>
  • At the lateral epicondyle it lies deeply between brachialis and brachioradialis where it then divides into a superficial and deep terminal branch.<\/li>
  • Deep branch crosses the supinator to become the posterior interosseous nerve.<\/li><\/ul>
