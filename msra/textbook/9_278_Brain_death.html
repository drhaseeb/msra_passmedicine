    Testing for brain death
    • Fixed pupils which do not respond to sharp changes in the intensity of incident light<\/li>
    • No corneal reflex<\/li>
    • Absent oculo-vestibular reflexes - no eye movements following the slow injection of at least 50ml of ice-cold water into each ear in turn (the caloric test)<\/li>
    • No response to supraorbital pressure<\/li>
    • No cough reflex to bronchial stimulation or gagging response to pharyngeal stimulation<\/li>
    • No observed respiratory effort in response to disconnection of the ventilator for long enough (typically 5 minutes) to ensure elevation of the arterial partial pressure of carbon dioxide to at least 6.0 kPa (6.5 kPa in patients with chronic carbon dioxide retention). Adequate oxygenation is ensured by pre-oxygenation and diffusion oxygenation during the disconnection (so the brain stem respiratory centre is not challenged by the ultimate, anoxic, drive stimulus)<\/li><\/ul>
      The test should be undertaken by two appropriately experienced doctors on two separate occasions. Both should be experienced in performing brain stem death testing and have at least 5 years post-graduate experience. One of them must be a consultant.
