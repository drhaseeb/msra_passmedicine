<\/li><\/ul>Breast cyst<\/td>
  • 7% of all Western females will present with a breast cyst<\/li>
  • Usually presents as a smooth discrete lump (may be fluctuant)<\/li>
  • Small increased risk of breast cancer (especially if younger)<\/td>
Cysts should be aspirated, those which are blood stained or persistently refill should be biopsied or excised<\/td><\/tr>
<\/li><\/ul>Sclerosing adenosis, (radial scars and complex sclerosing lesions)<\/td>
  • Usually presents as a breast lump or breast pain<\/li>
  • Causes mammographic changes which may mimic carcinoma<\/li>
  • Cause distortion of the distal lobular unit, without hyperplasia (complex lesions will show hyperplasia)<\/li>
  • Considered a disorder of involution, no increase in malignancy risk<\/td>
Lesions should be biopsied, excision is not mandatory<\/td><\/tr>

  • Atypical features and family history of breast cancer confers greatly increased risk of malignancy<\/td>
<\/li><\/ul>If no atypical features then conservative, those with atypical features require either close monitoring or surgical resection<\/td><\/tr>
