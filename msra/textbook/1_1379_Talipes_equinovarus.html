Talipes equinovarus is twice as common in males than females and has an incidence of 1 per 1,000 births. Around 50% of cases are bilateral.

Most commonly idiopathic. Associations include:
  • spina bifida<\/li>
  • cerebral palsy<\/li>
  • Edward's syndrome (trisomy 18)<\/li>
  • oligohydramnios<\/li>
  • arthrogryposis<\/li><\/ul>

    Management*
    • in recent years there has been a move away from surgical intervention to more conservative methods such as the Ponseti method<\/li>
    • the Ponseti method consists of manipulation and progressive casting which starts soon after birth. The deformity is usually corrected after 6-10 weeks. An Achilles tenotomy is required in around 85% of cases but this can usually be done under local anaesthetic<\/li>
    • night-time braces should be applied until the child is aged 4 years. The relapse rate is 15%<\/li><\/ul>
