Whilst it was previously thought HSV-1 accounted for oral lesions (cold sores) and HSV-2 for genital herpes it is now known there is considerable overlap

Features
  • primary infection: may present with a severe gingivostomatitis<\/li>
  • cold sores<\/li>
  • painful genital ulceration<\/li><\/ul>
    Management
    • gingivostomatitis: oral aciclovir, chlorhexidine mouthwash<\/li>
    • cold sores: topical aciclovir although the evidence base for this is modest<\/li>
    • genital herpes: oral aciclovir. Some patients with frequent exacerbations may benefit from longer term aciclovir<\/li><\/ul>
      Pregnancy
      • elective caesarean section at term is advised if a primary attack of herpes occurs during pregnancy at greater than 28 weeks gestation<\/li>
      • women with recurrent herpes who are pregnant should be treated with suppressive therapy and be advised that the risk of transmission to their baby is low<\/li><\/ul>
