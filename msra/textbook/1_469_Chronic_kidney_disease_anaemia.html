Causes of anaemia in renal failure
  • reduced erythropoietin levels<\/span>
    • the most significant factor in CKD causing anaemia<\/li>
    • CKD is associated with a decrease in the production of erythropoietin, a hormone produced by the kidneys that stimulates erythropoiesis in the bone marrow<\/li>
    • reduced erythropoietin levels → diminished red blood cell production<\/li><\/ul><\/li>
    • reduced absorption of iron

      • elevated hepcidin levels<\/span> lead to decreased iron absorption from the gut and impaired release of stored iron from macrophages and hepatocytes, reducing the iron available for erythropoiesis<\/li>

      • reduced red cell survival (especially in haemodialysis)<\/li>
      • blood loss due to capillary fragility and poor platelet function<\/li>
      • stress ulceration leading to chronic blood loss<\/li><\/ul>
