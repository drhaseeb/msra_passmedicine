Types of injury<\/h5>
Ruptured anterior cruciate ligament<\/b><\/td>
  • Sport injury<\/li>
  • Mechanism: high twisting force applied to a bent knee<\/li>
  • Typically presents with: loud crack, pain and RAPID joint swelling (haemoarthrosis)<\/li>
  • Poor healing<\/li>
  • Management: intense physiotherapy or surgery<\/td><\/tr>
<\/li><\/ul>Ruptured posterior cruciate ligament<\/b><\/td>
  • Mechanism: hyperextension injuries<\/li>
  • Tibia lies back on the femur<\/li>
  • Paradoxical anterior draw test<\/td><\/tr>
<\/li><\/ul>Rupture of medial collateral ligament<\/b><\/td>
  • Mechanism: leg forced into valgus via force outside the leg<\/li>
  • Knee unstable when put into valgus position<\/td><\/tr>
<\/li><\/ul>Menisceal tear<\/b><\/td>
  • Rotational sporting injuries<\/li>
  • Delayed knee swelling<\/li>
  • Joint locking (Patient may develop skills to 'unlock' the knee<\/li>
  • Recurrent episodes of pain and effusions are common, often following minor trauma<\/td><\/tr>
<\/li><\/ul>Chondromalacia patellae<\/b><\/td>
  • Teenage girls, following an injury to knee e.g. Dislocation patella<\/li>
  • Typical history of pain on going downstairs or at rest<\/li>
  • Tenderness, quadriceps wasting<\/td><\/tr>
<\/li><\/ul>Dislocation of the patella<\/b><\/td>
  • Most commonly occurs as a traumatic primary event, either through direct trauma or through severe contraction of quadriceps with knee stretched in valgus and external rotation<\/li>
  • Genu valgum, tibial torsion and high riding patella are risk factors<\/li>
  • Skyline x-ray views of patella are required, although displaced patella may be clinically obvious<\/li>
  • An osteochondral fracture is present in 5%<\/li>
  • The condition has a 20% recurrence rate<\/td><\/tr>
<\/li><\/ul>Fractured patella<\/b><\/td>
  • 2 types: <\/li><\/ul>i. Direct blow to patella causing undisplaced fragments
    ii. Avulsion fracture<\/td><\/tr>
Tibial plateau fracture<\/b><\/td>
  • Occur in the elderly (or following significant trauma in young)<\/li>
  • Mechanism: knee forced into valgus or varus, but the knee fractures before the ligaments rupture<\/li>
  • Varus injury affects medial plateau and if valgus injury, lateral plateau depressed fracture occurs<\/li>
  • Classified using the Schatzker system (see below)<\/td><\/tr><\/tbody><\/table><\/div><\/li><\/ul>
