Rinne's test
  • tuning fork is placed over the mastoid process until the sound is no longer heard, followed by repositioning just over external acoustic meatus<\/li>
  • 'positive test': air conduction (AC) is normally better than bone conduction (BC)<\/li>
  • 'negative test': if BC > AC then conductive deafness<\/span><\/li><\/ul>
    Weber's test
    • tuning fork is placed in the middle of the forehead equidistant from the patient's ears<\/li>
    • the patient is then asked which side is loudest<\/li>
    • in unilateral sensorineural deafness, sound is localised to the unaffected side<\/li>
    • in unilateral conductive deafness, sound is localised to the affected side<\/li><\/ul>
