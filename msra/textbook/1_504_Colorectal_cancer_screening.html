      Faecal Immunochemical Test (FIT) screening<\/h5>
      Key points
      • the NHS now has a national screening programme offering screening every 2 years to all men and women aged 50 to 74 years<\/span>. Patients aged over 74 years may request screening<\/span><\/li>
      • eligible patients are sent Faecal Immunochemical Test (FIT) tests through the post<\/li>
      • a type of faecal occult blood (FOB) test which uses antibodies that specifically recognise human haemoglobin (Hb)<\/li>
      • used to detect, and can quantify, the amount of human blood in a single stool sample<\/li>
      • advantages over conventional FOB tests is that it only detects human haemoglobin<\/span>, as opposed to animal haemoglobin ingested through diet<\/li>
      • only one faecal sample is needed compared to the 2-3 for conventional FOB tests<\/li>
      • whilst a numerical value is generated, this is not reported to the patient or GP, who will instead be informed if the test is normal or abnormal<\/li>
      • patients with abnormal results are offered a colonoscopy<\/li><\/ul>
        At colonoscopy, approximately:
        • 5 out of 10 patients will have a normal exam<\/li>
        • 4 out of 10 patients will be found to have polyps which may be removed due to their premalignant potential<\/li>
