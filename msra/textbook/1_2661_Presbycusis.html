[{"notes_id":"1_2661","category":"2","subcategory":"2","title":"Presbycusis","body":"Presbycusis is a type of sensorineural hearing loss that affects elderly individuals. Typically, high-frequency hearing is affected bilaterally, which can lead to conversational difficulties, particularly in noisy environments. Presbycusis progresses slowly, as sensory hair cells and neurons in the cochlea atrophy over time. Although certain factors are associated with presbycusis, it is distinct from noise-related hearing loss.

Epidemiology
  • In the USA, it is estimated that 25-30% of 65-74 year-olds have impaired hearing. For those over 75 years, the incidence is 40-50%<\/li>
