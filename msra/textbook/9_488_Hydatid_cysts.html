They are caused by the tapeworm parasite Echinococcus granulosus<\/i>. An outer fibrous capsule is formed containing multiple small daughter cysts. These cysts are allergens which precipitate a type 1 hypersensitivity reaction<\/b>.

Clinical features are as follows:
  • Up to 90% of cysts occur in the liver and lungs <\/li>
  • Can be asymptomatic, or symptomatic if cysts > 5cm in diameter<\/li>
  • Morbidity caused by cyst bursting, infection and organ dysfunction (biliary, bronchial, renal and cerebrospinal fluid outflow obstruction)<\/li>
  • In biliary rupture, there may be the classical triad of; biliary colic, jaundice, and urticaria <\/li><\/ul>

      • CT is the best investigation to differentiate hydatid cysts from amoebic and pyogenic cysts<\/li><\/ul><\/li>
      • serology<\/span>
        • useful for primary diagnosis and for follow-up after treatment<\/li>
        • wide variety of different antibody\/antigen tests available<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>
