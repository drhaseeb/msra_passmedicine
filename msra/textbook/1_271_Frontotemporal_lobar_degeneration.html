    Pick's disease<\/h5>
    This is the most common type and is characterised by personality change and impaired social conduct<\/span>. Other common features include hyperorality, disinhibition, increased appetite, and perseveration behaviours.

    Focal gyral atrophy with a knife-blade appearance is characteristic of Pick's disease.

    Macroscopic changes seen in Pick's disease include:-
    • Atrophy of the frontal and temporal lobes<\/li><\/ul>
      Microscopic changes include:-
      • Pick bodies - spherical aggregations of tau protein (silver-staining)<\/li>
      • Gliosis<\/li>
      • Neurofibrillary tangles<\/li>
      • Senile plaques<\/li><\/ul>
        Management
        • NICE do not recommend that AChE inhibitors or memantine are used in people with frontotemporal dementia<\/li><\/ul>
          CPA<\/h5>
          Here the chief factor is non fluent speech. They make short utterances that are agrammatic. Comprehension is relatively preserved.

          Semantic dementia<\/h5>
          Here the patient has a fluent progressive aphasia. The speech is fluent but empty and conveys little meaning.
