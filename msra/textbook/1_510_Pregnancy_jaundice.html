Intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy<\/h5>
Intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy (also known as obstetric cholestasis) occurs in around 1% of pregnancies and is generally seen in the third trimester. It is the most common liver disease of pregnancy.

Features
  • pruritus, often in the palms and soles<\/li>
  • no rash (although skin changes may be seen due to scratching)<\/li>
  • raised bilirubin<\/li><\/ul>
    Management
    • ursodeoxycholic acid is used for symptomatic relief<\/li>
    • weekly liver function tests<\/li>
    • women are typically induced at 37 weeks<\/li><\/ul>
      Complications include an increased rate of stillbirth. It is not generally associated with increased maternal morbidity


      Acute fatty liver of pregnancy<\/h5>
      Acute fatty liver of pregnancy is rare complication which may occur in the third trimester or the period immediately following delivery.

      Features
      • abdominal pain<\/li>
      • nausea & vomiting<\/li>
      • headache<\/li>
      • jaundice<\/li>
      • hypoglycaemia<\/li>
      • severe disease may result in pre-eclampsia<\/li><\/ul>
        Investigations
        • ALT is typically elevated e.g. 500 u\/l<\/li><\/ul>
          Management

          • once stabilised delivery is the definitive management<\/li><\/ul>

            Gilbert's, Dubin-Johnson syndrome, may be exacerbated during pregnancy
