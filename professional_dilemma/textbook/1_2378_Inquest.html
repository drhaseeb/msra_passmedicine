[{"notes_id":"1_2378","category":"1","subcategory":"0","title":"Inquest","body":"An inquest is a public enquiry into a death, held at Coroner's Court. You may be required to attend an inquest at some point during your career to give evidence about your involvement in the care of a patient who died. It is a legal proceeding but not a criminal one - it cannot apportion blame or responsibility to a person or body; its purpose is to determine who, where, when and how. However, like a criminal proceeding, you may be questioned by a lawyer representing the patient, there may be a jury, and the press may be present.
