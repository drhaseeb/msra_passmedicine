The duty of candour relates to paragraph 6 - 21 in the GMC's Good Medical Practice which states: 'Your duty to be open and honest with patients in your care, or those close to them, if something goes wrong. The General Medical Council (GMC) have issued guidance on how to be a doctor who talks to patients with candour when events have not gone according to plan, when mistakes are made and when harm is caused.

The four main suggestions are:
  • Talk to the patient and admit the error or situation<\/li>
  • Explain why the situation has happened<\/li>
  • Apologise to the patient
